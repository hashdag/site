I’m a PhD candidate at the Rachel and Selim Benin School of Computer Science and Engineering at the Faculty of Science, the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.

My research area includes Networks, Algorithmic Game Theory, and Bitcoin, under the supervision of Aviv Zohar, a senior lecturer at the school. I’m also a big fan of Probability Theory.

Currently, my projects with Aviv focus on Bitcoin, analyzing its security, throughput and incentive dynamics, and suggesting and investigating protocol modifications which enhance its performance. Specific aspects include:

  • Analyzing the growth rate of the Bitcoin ledger under general topologies of the network.

  • Understanding “selfish mining” strategies and possible countermeasures.

  • The GHOST protocol, a modification that significantly enhances the original protocol efficiency and security under nonnegligible delays.

  • The Inclusive feature, another protocol modification, which enables and incentivizes miners to increase the throughput.
